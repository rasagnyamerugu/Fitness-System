Legs & Glutes

Strong legs and glutes are the foundation of athletic performance and a balanced physique. These exercises target your quads, hamstrings, glutes, and calves for complete lower body development.

1. Barbell Back Squat

The squat is the king of all lower body exercises. Place a barbell across your upper back, stand with feet shoulder-width apart, and descend until your thighs are at least parallel to the floor. Drive through your heels to stand back up. Aim for 4 sets of 5–8 reps for strength, or 3 sets of 10–12 for hypertrophy.

Muscles worked: Quadriceps, Glutes, Hamstrings, Core, Spinal erectors

2. Romanian Deadlift (RDL)

The RDL is one of the best exercises for hamstring and glute development. Hold a barbell with an overhand grip, hinge at the hips while keeping your back flat, and lower the bar along your legs until you feel a strong stretch in your hamstrings. Drive your hips forward to return to standing. Do 3–4 sets of 8–12 reps.

Muscles worked: Hamstrings, Glutes, Spinal erectors

3. Hip Thrust

The hip thrust is arguably the most effective glute isolation exercise. Rest your upper back on a bench, place a barbell across your hips, and drive your hips upward until your body forms a straight line from knees to shoulders. Squeeze your glutes at the top. Perform 3–4 sets of 10–15 reps.

Muscles worked: Glutes (all heads), Hamstrings, Core

4. Walking Lunges

Walking lunges build single-leg strength and improve balance. Step forward with one leg, lower your back knee toward the floor, then push off and bring the back leg forward to take the next lunge step. Hold dumbbells for added resistance. Do 3 sets of 10–12 reps per leg.

Muscles worked: Quadriceps, Glutes, Hamstrings, Calves

5. Leg Press

The leg press is a machine-based compound exercise that lets you load the quads and glutes heavily with reduced spinal load. Place your feet on the platform at shoulder width, lower the sled until your knees reach 90 degrees, then press back up without locking your knees. Perform 3–4 sets of 10–15 reps.

Muscles worked: Quadriceps, Glutes, Hamstrings

Tips for Legs & Glutes Training

  • Always warm up with bodyweight squats and hip circles before loading up.
  • Focus on a full range of motion — partial reps limit muscle activation.
  • Train legs at least twice a week for best results.
  • Don't skip calves — include standing or seated calf raises at the end of your session.
  • Progressive overload is key — aim to increase weight or reps each week.
