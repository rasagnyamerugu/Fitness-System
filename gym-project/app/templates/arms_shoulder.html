Arms & Shoulders

Build strong, defined arms and boulder shoulders with these essential exercises. Whether you're training for size, strength, or endurance, this guide covers everything you need.

1. Barbell Bicep Curl

The barbell curl is a classic mass-builder for the biceps. Stand with feet shoulder-width apart, grip the barbell with an underhand grip, and curl it up to shoulder height while keeping your elbows pinned to your sides. Slowly lower it back down. Aim for 3–4 sets of 8–12 reps.

Muscles worked: Biceps brachii, Brachialis

2. Tricep Dips

Tricep dips effectively target the back of the upper arm. Using parallel bars or a bench, lower your body by bending the elbows to about 90 degrees, then push back up. Keep your torso upright for more tricep activation. Perform 3 sets of 10–15 reps.

Muscles worked: Triceps brachii, Anterior deltoid, Pectorals

3. Overhead Shoulder Press

The overhead press is a fundamental compound movement for building shoulder size and strength. Press a barbell or dumbbells from shoulder height straight overhead until your arms are fully extended. Lower back down in a controlled manner. Do 3–4 sets of 6–10 reps.

Muscles worked: Anterior deltoid, Lateral deltoid, Trapezius, Triceps

4. Lateral Raises

Lateral raises isolate the side (lateral) head of the deltoid, giving your shoulders that wide, capped look. Hold a dumbbell in each hand, and raise both arms out to the sides until they're at shoulder height. Lower slowly. Perform 3 sets of 12–15 reps with light to moderate weight.

Muscles worked: Lateral deltoid, Supraspinatus

5. Hammer Curls

Hammer curls target the brachialis and brachioradialis, adding thickness to the arm. Hold dumbbells with a neutral (palms-facing-in) grip and curl them up alternately or simultaneously. This also helps build forearm strength. Do 3 sets of 10–12 reps per arm.

Muscles worked: Brachialis, Brachioradialis, Biceps brachii

Tips for Arms & Shoulders Training

  • Always warm up your shoulder joints before heavy pressing movements.
  • Don't neglect the rear deltoids — include face pulls or reverse flyes.
  • Use a full range of motion on curls for maximum bicep activation.
  • Keep ego in check on lateral raises — lighter weight with strict form beats heavy and sloppy.
  • Train arms 2x per week for optimal muscle growth.
