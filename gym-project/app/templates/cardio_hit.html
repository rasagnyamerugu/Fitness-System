Cardio & HIIT

Cardiovascular training and High-Intensity Interval Training (HIIT) are powerful tools for burning fat, improving endurance, and boosting heart health. Whether you prefer steady-state cardio or explosive intervals, this guide has you covered.

1. Jump Rope (Skipping)

Jumping rope is one of the most efficient and accessible cardio exercises available. It burns a high number of calories per minute while also improving coordination and footwork. Start with 3 rounds of 2–3 minutes with 60 seconds rest between rounds. Gradually build up your time as your fitness improves.

Benefits: High calorie burn, coordination, calf and shoulder endurance

2. Burpees

Burpees are a full-body HIIT staple that combine a squat, push-up, and jump into one explosive movement. From standing, drop into a squat, kick your feet back into a push-up position, perform a push-up, jump your feet forward, then leap into the air with your arms overhead. Perform 4 rounds of 10–15 reps with 30 seconds rest.

Benefits: Full-body conditioning, fat burning, cardiovascular endurance

3. Sprint Intervals

Sprint intervals are one of the most effective methods for burning fat and building explosive speed. On a treadmill or outdoors, sprint at maximum effort for 20–30 seconds, then walk or slow jog for 60–90 seconds to recover. Repeat for 6–10 rounds. This style of training elevates your metabolism for hours after the session.

Benefits: Fat loss, VO2 max improvement, speed and power development

4. Cycling (Stationary Bike)

Stationary cycling is a low-impact cardio option that's easy on the joints. Use it for steady-state cardio (30–60 minutes at moderate effort) or interval training (alternate between high resistance sprints and easy recovery pedaling). It's particularly useful for active recovery days or when your legs are sore from heavy training.

Benefits: Low-impact cardio, leg endurance, active recovery

5. Mountain Climbers

Mountain climbers are a dynamic core and cardio exercise performed in a push-up position. Drive one knee toward your chest, then quickly switch legs in a running motion while keeping your hips level and core tight. Perform for time: 4 rounds of 30–45 seconds with 20 seconds rest. The faster you go, the higher your heart rate climbs.

Benefits: Core strength, cardiovascular conditioning, full-body engagement

Tips for Cardio & HIIT Training

  • Don't do intense HIIT every day — 2–3 sessions per week with rest or steady cardio in between is optimal.
  • Always warm up for 5 minutes with light movement before starting high-intensity work.
  • Stay hydrated — HIIT causes significant sweat loss.
  • Track your heart rate to make sure you're working in the right zone (80–95% max HR for HIIT intervals).
  • Combine HIIT with strength training for the best body composition results.
