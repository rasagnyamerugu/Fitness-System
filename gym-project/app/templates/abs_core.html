Abs & Core

A strong core is not just about aesthetics — it's essential for posture, injury prevention, and overall athletic performance. These exercises target your entire core, from the rectus abdominis to the deep stabilizing muscles.

1. Plank

The plank is a foundational isometric core exercise. Get into a push-up position and hold with your forearms on the ground, body forming a straight line from head to heels. Brace your core and squeeze your glutes. Start with 3 holds of 30–60 seconds and work your way up over time.

Muscles worked: Transverse abdominis, Rectus abdominis, Obliques, Glutes, Shoulder stabilizers

2. Cable Crunch

The cable crunch is one of the best-loaded exercises for the rectus abdominis. Kneel in front of a cable machine, hold the rope attachment behind your head, and crunch downward by flexing your spine — not just pulling with your arms. Squeeze your abs at the bottom. Do 3–4 sets of 12–15 reps.

Muscles worked: Rectus abdominis, Obliques

3. Hanging Leg Raises

Hanging leg raises are excellent for targeting the lower abs and hip flexors. Hang from a pull-up bar and raise your legs until they are parallel to the floor (or higher for more challenge). Lower them slowly without swinging. Perform 3 sets of 10–15 reps. Bend your knees if straight legs are too difficult at first.

Muscles worked: Lower rectus abdominis, Hip flexors, Obliques

4. Russian Twists

Russian twists develop rotational strength in the obliques. Sit on the floor, lean back slightly, and lift your feet off the ground. Rotate your torso from side to side, touching the floor (or a weight) next to each hip. For added difficulty, hold a plate or medicine ball. Do 3 sets of 20 total reps (10 per side).

Muscles worked: Obliques, Rectus abdominis, Hip flexors

5. Ab Wheel Rollout

The ab wheel rollout is one of the most challenging core exercises. Kneel on the floor, hold the ab wheel with both hands, and slowly roll forward as far as you can while keeping your core tight and back flat. Pull back using your core to return. Start with short range rollouts and progress over time. Aim for 3 sets of 8–10 reps.

Muscles worked: Rectus abdominis, Transverse abdominis, Lats, Shoulder stabilizers

Tips for Abs & Core Training

  • Abs are made in the kitchen — diet and low body fat are key to visible abs.
  • Train your core 3–4 times per week; it recovers faster than most muscle groups.
  • Include both flexion exercises (crunches) and anti-flexion/stability exercises (planks, rollouts).
  • Don't neglect the obliques — side planks and twisting movements are essential.
  • Quality over quantity — slow, controlled reps beat fast, sloppy ones every time.
